Mercury

Mercury is the closest and smallest planet to the sun in the solar system. Due to its proximity to the sun, it experiences extreme temperature fluctuations from -173 °C to 427 °C. It has no atmosphere, which results in a crater-rich surface that is very similar to that of the moon.

Venus

Venus is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" as its size and composition are similar. The dense atmosphere of carbon dioxide and the clouds of sulphuric acid lead to an extreme greenhouse effect, which raises the surface temperatures to up to 470 °C. Venus has no moons and is red. Venus has no moons and rotates in the opposite direction to most other planets.
